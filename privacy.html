Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 13, 2026

We don't collect personal data. We use Google Analytics for anonymous usage stats and Google AdSense for ads. No account required. No cookies set by us directly.

This privacy policy describes how Bash Snippets ("this site", "we") handles information when you visit this website. The short version: we collect as little as possible and don't sell anything to anyone.

What information is collected

This site does not require account creation, login, or any personal information to use. However, the following may be collected automatically:

  • Server / hosting logs: GitHub Pages (our hosting provider) may log basic access data such as IP address, browser type, and pages visited. We do not access or control these logs directly. See GitHub's data collection policy.
  • Google AdSense (if active): If advertisements are displayed, Google AdSense may set cookies and collect data to serve relevant ads. Google's use of advertising cookies is described in their advertising privacy policy.
  • Google Analytics (if active): We may use Google Analytics to understand how pages are being used. This collects anonymised usage data. You can opt out using the Google Analytics Opt-out Add-on.

Cookies

This site itself does not set first-party cookies. Third-party services (Google AdSense, Google Analytics) may set their own cookies. You can control cookie behaviour through your browser settings or by using a browser extension like uBlock Origin.

Affiliate Links

Some links on this site may be affiliate links. This means if you click a link and make a purchase, we may earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. Affiliate links are labelled or noted near the link. We only link to products we would recommend regardless of commission.

Examples include referrals to DigitalOcean and Namecheap where those partners offer programs we participate in. Those links are clearly labeled as affiliate or referral links where they appear.

Children's privacy

This site is not directed at children under the age of 13 and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Your rights

Since we don't collect personal data directly, there is typically nothing to request deletion of. If you have concerns about data collected by third-party services (Google, GitHub), please refer to their respective privacy policies linked above.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated date at the top of this page will reflect that. We don't plan on major changes — this is a simple static website.
